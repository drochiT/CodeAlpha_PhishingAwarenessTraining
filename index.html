Hacking the Human: Social Engineering

Before the "Phish" comes the "Hook." Social engineering is the psychological manipulation used to trick people into divulging confidential info.

Pretexting

Creating a fake story (e.g., "I'm from IT") to build trust.

Baiting

Using a "trap" like a free gift or USB to infect systems.

Quid Pro Quo

Promising a favor in exchange for sensitive data.

Spotting Phishing Emails

⚠️ Urgent Language

"Your account will be deleted in 2 hours!"

📧 Mismatched Domains

support@google-security-fix.com (Not google.com)

📎 Suspicious Links

Hidden URLs that lead to malicious login pages.

Knowledge Check

Think you can spot a scam? Let's test your instincts.

Q1: Which of the following is a major phishing sign?

A. An official email from your manager.
B. An urgent request to "verify" your password immediately.
C. A website using HTTPS encryption.

✓ Correct Answer: B (Legitimate companies never ask for passwords via email.)

Q2: What should you do before clicking a link in an email?

A. Click it quickly to see where it goes.
B. Hover your mouse over the link to inspect the actual URL.
C. Ignore all browser warnings and proceed.

✓ Correct Answer: B (Always verify the destination before clicking.)

Best Practices to Stay Safe

  • Enable MFA: Use Multi-Factor Authentication everywhere.
  • Think Before You Click: Stay skeptical of unexpected attachments.
  • Update Software: Keep your browser and OS patched.
  • Report: Use your company's "Report Phish" button.

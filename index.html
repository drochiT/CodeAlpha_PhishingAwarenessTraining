What is Phishing?

Phishing is a cyberattack where criminals impersonate trusted organizations or individuals to steal sensitive information such as passwords, banking credentials, or personal data.

Email Phishing

Fraudulent emails pretending to be from banks, companies, or trusted services.

Spear Phishing

Targeted phishing attacks aimed at specific individuals or organizations.

Smishing & Vishing

Phishing through SMS messages or phone calls designed to steal information.

Social Engineering Tactics

Attackers often exploit human psychology rather than technology vulnerabilities.

Authority

Attackers pretend to be managers, banks, or IT departments.

Urgency

Messages create panic to force quick reactions without thinking.

Fear & Curiosity

Scammers use fear or exciting offers to manipulate victims.

How to Spot Phishing Emails

⚠ Suspicious Sender

Emails from unknown or slightly altered domains.

🚨 Urgent Messages

"Your account will be suspended immediately!"

📎 Malicious Attachments

Unexpected files asking you to download or enable macros.

🔗 Fake Links

Links redirecting users to fraudulent websites.

Recognizing Fake Websites

Misspelled URLs

Example: g00gle.com instead of google.com

No HTTPS

A missing padlock icon may indicate an unsafe site.

Poor Website Design

Broken layouts, grammar mistakes, and suspicious popups.

Phishing Attack Flow

Understanding how a phishing attack works can help you stop it before damage occurs.

1️⃣ Attacker Sends Fake Email
➜
2️⃣ Victim Clicks Link
➜
3️⃣ Fake Website Opens
➜
4️⃣ Credentials Are Stolen

Real-World Phishing Examples

Bank Scam

Fake bank emails asking users to verify accounts through malicious links.

Delivery Scam

Messages claiming a package delivery problem with fake tracking links.

Workplace Scam

Emails impersonating HR or company executives requesting urgent payments.

Best Practices to Stay Safe

  • Enable MFA: Add an extra security layer using Multi-Factor Authentication.
  • Verify Requests: Confirm suspicious messages through official channels.
  • Think Before Clicking: Avoid rushing when reading emails or messages.
  • Use Strong Passwords: Create unique passwords for every account.
  • Keep Systems Updated: Install software updates and security patches regularly.
  • Report Suspicious Emails: Notify your IT or security department immediately.
